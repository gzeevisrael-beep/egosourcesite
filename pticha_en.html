How to read this guide

Baal HaSulam isn't describing physical cosmology. He describes an order of cause and effect between Light, the will to receive, and intention. Words such as world, head, body, mouth, ascent, and descent belong to the language of branches. They point to spiritual qualities and calculations.

Basic rule

Higher means greater similarity to bestowal. Lower means stronger rule of reception for oneself. Near means similarity of form. Far means difference of form.

Method of study

  1. Read the main idea of the lesson from beginning to end.
  2. Return to the definitions and the sequence.
  3. Check whether a bodily or spatial image has entered your understanding.
  4. Connect the law being studied with the goal, the revelation of the Creator in corrected connection.
  5. End with a request for the Light that reforms.

Practical precision according to Rabash

A person doesn't create a screen through willpower and doesn't correct desire by suppressing it. One builds an environment, raises the importance of the goal, tries to be included in the friends, discovers the inability to leave self concern, and turns to the Creator. The correcting force comes from above.

01The Thought of Creation and the substance of creationOpen

Source reference: Items 1 and 4

The Thought of Creation

The entire process begins with the Creator's desire to delight the created beings. From the side of the Creator there is bestowal, fulfillment, and love. For delight to be felt, there must be a being that wants to receive it.

The will to receive is the Kli that determines the measure of pleasure, neither less nor more.

Pleasure by itself doesn't create sensation. Sensation appears only in desire. Creation therefore begins with a new quality, the desire to receive what comes from the Creator.

What does something from nothing mean

Light belongs to what comes from the Creator. The will to receive doesn't exist in the quality of bestowal, so it is called something from nothing. This doesn't describe a physical object appearing from empty space. It means that a genuinely new quality appears, one that is opposite to bestowal.

The central distinction

On the side of the Creator there is a desire to bestow. On the side of creation there is a desire to receive. Everything that follows develops from the relationship between these two qualities.

The entire substance of creation

Baal HaSulam defines the will to receive as the whole substance of creation from beginning to end. Created beings differ in the size of desire, its form, its intention, and the fulfillment toward which it is directed.

The still, vegetative, animate, and human levels are different expressions of desire. In a person, desire develops from bodily needs to wealth, honor, control, knowledge, and finally the question about the meaning of life.

Precision

The statement that everything is desire doesn't deny the material reality we perceive. It says that the entire attained picture is revealed inside desire and is shaped by its response to the Light.

Connection with the Creator

In material pleasure a person feels the taste, but doesn't attain the source that formed the desire and the fulfillment. In spirituality, the attainment of the Giver, His attitude, and His purpose is added to the pleasure.

The sensation of the source of pleasure is called the revelation of the Creator. It isn't a visual image. It is the attainment of the quality of bestowal within a corrected Kli.

Spiritual and material

Material refers to desire in an egoistic form, when it feels only itself and the fulfillment inside itself. Spiritual refers to a relation in which desire acquires an intention to bestow and, through similarity of form, begins to attain the Creator.

Similarity of form

Spirituality isn't revealed by moving to another place. It is revealed by acquiring a quality similar to the Light. Desire senses the Creator to the extent that it resembles bestowal.

Practical direction

There is no need to fight ordinary desires. It is useful to observe that every movement begins with an expectation of fulfillment, and then to ask through the environment that the desire for spirituality become more important than remaining enclosed in oneself.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

The Creator created evil by creating the will to receive.

Correct direction

Desire itself is the substance of creation. Evil is revealed in the intention to use desire only for oneself and against connection.

Don't understand it this way

Revelation of the Creator is an unusual vision.

Correct direction

Revelation means attaining the quality of bestowal and the attitude of the Giver within corrected desire.

Lesson summary

  1. The purpose of creation is to delight the created beings.
  2. The will to receive was created so delight could be felt.
  3. Desire is the one new substance of creation.
  4. Spirituality begins with attainment of the Giver through similarity of form.
  5. The intention in the use of desire is what must be corrected.
02The four phases of Direct LightOpen

Source reference: Items 5 through 12

Why four phases are needed

Desire doesn't become independent at once. In its first response it is completely determined by the Light. For the created being to truly want fulfillment, it must pass through impressions of reception, the quality of the Giver, refusal, and measured reception.

General sequence

Root, Keter. Phase one, Hochma. Phase two, Bina. Phase three, Zeir Anpin. Phase four, Malchut.

Keter is often called the root or phase zero. The four states that follow are called the four phases of desire. This is why five names and the phrase four phases appear together without contradiction.

Keter

Keter expresses the Creator's thought to delight the created being. There is no independent desire of the created being here. It is the root that contains the intention to bestow and the entire future process.

Hochma

The Light creates desire and immediately fills it. The desire receives, but doesn't yet feel itself as an independent receiver. It is fully governed by the Light.

Light of Hochma

The essential fulfillment, life, and pleasure that comes from the Creator.

Bina

Hochma begins to sense not only pleasure but also the quality of the Giver. A new desire appears, not to receive but to bestow. This state is called Bina.

Bina doesn't erase the will to receive. It prefers similarity with the Creator. Its fulfillment is the sensation of bestowal, called the Light of Hassadim.

Important precision

Bina isn't performing a moral act of self denial. The quality of bestowal is born in it through the action of the Light.

Zeir Anpin

Bina understands that the Creator wants the created being to receive His goodness. Complete refusal also doesn't fulfill the Thought of Creation. A combined state therefore appears, in which a small portion is received for the sake of the Giver.

Zeir Anpin includes six Sefirot, Hesed, Gevura, Tiferet, Netzah, Hod, and Yesod. This is how the four phases unfold into ten Sefirot.

Malchut

After the impressions of reception, refusal, and partial reception, a complete independent desire to receive all the Light appears. Malchut is therefore called the completed Kli.

The main difference between Malchut and Hochma is independence. Hochma receives because the Light created and filled it. Malchut wants to receive after knowing all the previous forms.

HaVaYaH and ten Sefirot

The four phases and their root form an unchanging pattern called HaVaYaH. Every spiritual object develops according to this order.

Why ten Sefirot

Keter, Hochma, Bina, the six Sefirot of Zeir Anpin, and Malchut form ten basic discernments.

Expansion and departure of the Light

For desire to become fit for independent work, the Light enters it, creates an impression, and then departs. After fulfillment is lost, an independent lack for the known pleasure appears.

The expansion of Light creates sensation. The departure of Light creates a Kli, a Reshimo, and an independent longing.

Practical direction

Spiritual desire is formed by the Light. A person therefore doesn't demand genuine bestowal from oneself immediately. One arranges study and connection so that the Light in the source can form a new desire.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

The four phases are events that happened in time.

Correct direction

They are a causal order of qualities. In spirituality cause is called earlier and effect is called later.

Don't understand it this way

Bina overcame desire by its own strength.

Correct direction

The quality of bestowal appeared as an impression from the Light and the Giver.

Lesson summary

  1. Keter is the root of the intention to bestow.
  2. Hochma receives without an independent demand.
  3. Bina wants similarity with the Giver.
  4. Zeir Anpin combines bestowal with measured reception.
  5. Malchut is the completed independent Kli.
  6. Every spiritual system follows the pattern of HaVaYaH.
03Shame, the First Restriction, and the screenOpen

Source reference: Items 14 through 16

Shame

Malchut of Ein Sof receives all the delight and at the same time senses the source of fulfillment. The difference of form becomes clear. The Creator bestows and Malchut receives.

Shame in Kabbalah isn't social embarrassment. It is attainment of the difference in qualities that separates the created being from the Creator.

Shame as the beginning of independence

The created being doesn't reject the purpose of receiving delight. It wants to receive in a form that doesn't separate it from the Creator.

The First Restriction

Malchut decides not to receive Light in desire for its own sake. This decision is called Tzimtzum Aleph, the First Restriction. The Light doesn't disappear and the Thought of Creation isn't cancelled. The condition of reception changes.

Law of the First Restriction

After the restriction, spiritual Light can't be received with an intention for oneself.

In this world a person receives small enlivening pleasures that allow life to continue before spiritual intention is acquired. The law of restriction shouldn't be applied mechanically to bodily life.

Why refusal isn't enough

If the created being only refuses the Light, the purpose of delighting creation won't be realized. A means is needed that allows reception while preserving similarity with the Giver. This means is the screen.

The screen

The screen is the force of intention above desire. It doesn't destroy Aviut. A great desire together with a strong screen makes it possible to receive great pleasure in order to bestow.

Two aspects are distinguished in the screen. Kashiut is resistance to direct reception. Aviut is the depth of desire over which the calculation is made.

Not a psychological prohibition

A screen isn't discipline, asceticism, or the ability to endure discomfort. It is a spiritual force that connects reception with the intention to delight the Creator.

Zivug de Hakaa

Direct Light presses upon the Kli according to the Thought of Creation. The screen rejects reception for oneself. This encounter is called Zivug de Hakaa, a coupling through striking.

The word striking describes opposition between two directions. The Light wants to fill. The Kli refuses egoistic reception. After the rejection, the Kli can measure how much may be received in order to bestow.

Reflected Light

The rejection of egoistic reception creates a new relation to the Giver. This relation is called Reflected Light. It rises from the Kli toward the source and expresses the created being's intention to bestow.

Reflected Light becomes the clothing for Direct Light. Pleasure can be received only within an intention to bestow.

The guest and the host

The host prepares a meal out of love. The guest feels both the desire to enjoy and shame at being only a receiver. First the guest refuses, showing that the meal doesn't rule him. Then, after understanding that the host wants his enjoyment, he receives for the host's sake.

Where the spiritual action is

The spiritual action isn't in the movement of the hand or the amount eaten. It is in the intention that changes the meaning of reception.

Practical direction

Don't practice a spiritual screen through artificial refusal of food or pleasure. According to Rabash, free effort is in the environment, raising the importance of bestowal, lowering the private calculation before the goal, caring for the friends' connection, and asking for the force of correction.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

Restriction means that desire disappears.

Correct direction

Desire remains in full strength. Only its egoistic use is restricted.

Don't understand it this way

A screen can be built through persuasion or self control.

Correct direction

A screen is the result of the reforming Light. A person prepares the need and the request.

Don't understand it this way

Shame is punishment.

Correct direction

Shame reveals the difference of form and allows the created being to desire independent similarity.

Lesson summary

  1. Shame is the sensation of opposition to the Giver.
  2. The First Restriction prevents spiritual reception for oneself.
  3. The screen preserves desire and changes how it is used.
  4. Zivug de Hakaa creates a measured relation between Light and Kli.
  5. Reflected Light is the intention to bestow and the clothing of Direct Light.
04Ten Sefirot, Partzuf, and the LightsOpen

Source reference: Items 17, 18, 26, 30, and 36

Three foundations

At this stage three foundations can be distinguished. First, Light and Kli. Second, ten Sefirot and worlds. Third, restriction and screen. All later details develop these relationships.

What are ten Sefirot

Ten Sefirot are ten basic forms of relationship between Light and desire. They aren't separate glowing spheres. A Sefira is a particular quality of the Kli in which Light is revealed according to similarity of form.

The same order of ten Sefirot is present in every spiritual object. Degrees differ by the depth of desire, the strength of the screen, and the kind of Light.

What is a Partzuf

A Partzuf is a system of desire that can calculate and receive a portion of Light in order to bestow. All its desires are connected by one purpose.

Structure of a Partzuf

Rosh makes the calculation. Toch receives in order to bestow. Sof contains desires that can't yet be used.

Rosh, Toch, and Sof

Rosh is the place of calculation. The Light hasn't yet been received into desire. The Partzuf measures the pleasure, Aviut, strength of screen, and intention. The place of the screen is called Peh, mouth.

Toch is the part of desire into which Light enters according to the calculation of the Rosh. The Light received in order to bestow is called Inner Light. The boundary of this reception is called Tabur.

Sof contains desires that the Partzuf can't use in order to bestow. They aren't destroyed. They remain empty under restriction until a stronger screen is acquired.

Inner and Surrounding Light

Inner Light is the portion of delight that the Kli actually receives in order to bestow. Surrounding Light is the Light intended for the Kli according to the purpose of creation but not yet able to enter because the screen is insufficient.

Surrounding Light presses on the boundary of the Kli and points toward full correction. Its influence leads to further development and the ending of the present state.

Weakening of the screen

After receiving Light, the screen gradually loses the power to maintain the previous level of Aviut. This is called Hizdakchut of the Masach, the purification or thinning of the screen.

This isn't an accidental failure. It allows one state to end, Reshimot to remain, and the next state to be born with a smaller measure of reception.

Language of roots and branches

The words head, body, mouth, and legs are used according to the relation between root and branch. A spiritual root determines the form of its material branch. We can't reverse the process and learn spirituality from anatomy.

Practical direction

Rosh can remind us of the need for prior intention, but ordinary thought isn't a spiritual Rosh. In the work of the ten, a person first clarifies the goal, checks whether an action serves connection, and asks that the result be bestowal rather than personal correctness.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

A Partzuf is a subtle body inside a person.

Correct direction

A Partzuf is a system of corrected desires and calculations.

Don't understand it this way

Surrounding Light is spatially located around the body.

Correct direction

It is called surrounding relative to a Kli that can't yet receive its destined fulfillment.

Don't understand it this way

Sof is a bad part.

Correct direction

Sof is a correct limit that shows the exact boundary of the present ability to bestow.

Lesson summary

  1. Ten Sefirot describe forms of connection between Light and desire.
  2. A Partzuf consists of Rosh, Toch, and Sof.
  3. Rosh calculates, Toch receives Inner Light, and Sof remains restricted.
  4. Surrounding Light expresses future fulfillment that can't yet enter.
  5. The weakening of the screen ends one state and prepares the next Partzuf.
05The birth of Partzufim and ReshimotOpen

Source reference: Items 26, 29, 46, and 49

The sequence of Partzufim

The first Partzuf receives according to the greatest calculation its screen can hold. The encounter between Inner and Surrounding Light weakens the screen. The previous state ends, but its information doesn't disappear.

On the basis of the remaining Reshimot a new Zivug de Hakaa is made and the next Partzuf is born. This is how the sequence of Partzufim in Adam Kadmon develops.

Reshimot

A Reshimo is an informational record of an experienced state. It contains data about the Light and about desire. Because of Reshimot, each new state is an exact consequence of the preceding one.

Two principal records

Reshimo de Hitlabshut contains the impression of the Light. Reshimo de Aviut contains information about the strength of desire.

Even after pleasure departs, desire retains a record of what was received and what kind of Kli it had. This record awakens a new lack and determines the next calculation.

Taamim, Nekudot, Tagin, Otiot

Taamim, Nekudot, Tagin, and Otiot describe a full cycle of relation between Light and Kli.

  1. Taamim, tastes. The expansion of Light and the main sensation of fulfillment.
  2. Nekudot, points. The states during the departure of Light as the screen weakens.
  3. Tagin, crowns. Additional records and illuminations connected with the departing state.
  4. Otiot, letters. The forms of the Kli that become clear after the Light leaves.

These names aren't only poetic. They express an exact record of the process by which a lower state can reproduce a spiritual action.

Why spiritual pleasure doesn't extinguish desire

In material reception, pleasure enters desire directly and reduces the lack. The enjoyment therefore fades. In spirituality, pleasure is received within an intention to bestow. Because the goal lies beyond private fulfillment, the connection can constantly renew itself.

The principle of unending pleasure

Desire receives for the sake of connection with the Giver rather than to fill itself. The greater the similarity, the greater the Kli and the pleasure that can be received without losing the purpose.

A new spiritual state

A new state isn't a copy of the old one. It appears after the previous degree is clarified, the Light departs, and the Reshimot awaken. Spiritual progress therefore includes fulfillment, weakening, emptiness, clarification, and new birth.

A descent in feeling doesn't always mean leaving the path. Sometimes the loss of the previous taste is necessary in order to reveal a new Reshimo and build a more independent relation.

Practical direction

In the work a person experiences taste, loss of taste, and a memory of the importance of the goal. Constant inspiration isn't required. The correct use of a Reshimo is to renew the importance of the goal through the environment and continue even without the previous fulfillment.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

Lack of taste proves that study isn't working.

Correct direction

The departure of taste can reveal a new Kli and the need for a more independent intention.

Don't understand it this way

A Reshimo is an ordinary memory in the brain.

Correct direction

A Reshimo is a spiritual informational gene that determines the development of desire.

Lesson summary

  1. The weakening of the screen ends the Partzuf's reception.
  2. Reshimot preserve information about Light and desire.
  3. A new Partzuf is born from the Reshimot of the previous state.
  4. Taamim, Nekudot, Tagin, and Otiot describe expansion, departure, and recording.
  5. Spiritual development includes both fulfillment and emptiness.
06The Second Restriction, Parsa, and the worldsOpen

Source reference: Items 56 through 64

Why the Second Restriction was needed

After the Partzufim of Adam Kadmon develop, a system is needed that can gradually include the deepest desires of Malchut in the quality of Bina and prepare them for correction.

The Second Restriction is based on the ascent of Malchut into Bina. The quality of judgment becomes included in the quality of mercy, and reception becomes included in bestowal.

Meaning of the inclusion

Uncorrected Malchut receives a connection with Bina, while Bina receives the ability to work for the correction of Malchut.

A new boundary

After Malchut is included in Bina, part of the desires that could previously be used under the First Restriction are now also limited. A new boundary for the expansion of Light appears.

This boundary is called Parsa. Below the Parsa are desires that can't yet be used in direct reception in order to bestow.

First and Second Restriction

AspectFirst RestrictionSecond Restriction
ReasonRefusal to receive for oneselfThe need to limit part of the desires after Malchut is included in Bina
Main lawReception is possible only in order to bestowSome desires can't yet be used even with an intention to bestow
ResultThe screen and Partzuf become possibleA new division of Kelim and the Parsa appear

Worlds as measures of concealment

The system of worlds is built so creation can approach the Creator gradually. A world is a measure of concealment of the Light and the quality of bestowal, not a location.

  1. Atzilut, the system of correction and divine governance.
  2. Beria, the first stronger degree of separation and concealment.
  3. Yetzira, a further reduction of revelation.
  4. Assiya, the strongest spiritual concealment.
  5. This world, the state in which the Creator is completely concealed from ordinary sensation.

Direction of movement

Descent from above downward builds concealments. Ascent from below upward means acquiring screens and revealing the same system from within.

What it means to raise desires above the Parsa

An ascent of desire means a change of its quality and its inclusion in an intention to bestow. Desire doesn't move in space. It becomes able to join the corrected system and receive Light.

Desires that can't yet be corrected remain under restriction. Correct work never means forcing an unprepared desire into use.

Practical direction

The Second Restriction teaches gradual work. A person isn't required to correct the deepest desires immediately. One works with what can presently be connected to bestowal, leaves the rest under restriction, and asks the upper system for preparation.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

The Parsa is a line in the cosmos.

Correct direction

It is a spiritual boundary in the use of particular desires.

Don't understand it this way

The worlds are parallel universes.

Correct direction

They are successive measures of concealment and revelation of the Creator's quality.

Don't understand it this way

The Second Restriction cancels the First.

Correct direction

It adds a new law and a new division while the First Restriction remains in force.

Lesson summary

  1. The Second Restriction comes through the inclusion of Malchut in Bina.
  2. The Parsa marks a new boundary in the use of desire.
  3. Worlds are measures of concealment of the Creator.
  4. Ascent means correction of form, not movement through space.
  5. Correction proceeds gradually from lighter desires to deeper ones.
07The breaking of the vessels and Adam HaRishonOpen

Source reference: Items 101 through 111

Why the breaking is necessary

Before the breaking there is a corrected bond, but creation hasn't achieved it independently from a state of complete opposition. To make freedom and conscious similarity possible, qualities of reception and bestowal must mix down to the deepest level.

Purpose of the breaking

To place a spark of bestowal within every egoistic desire and make correction possible through recognition of evil and an independent request.

What actually broke

Desires as substance didn't disappear. The bond between them broke, meaning the intention to bestow was lost. Each part began to experience itself separately and to use the others for itself.

Correction doesn't erase individuality. It restores the right connection above differences.

The world of Nekudim

In the technical description, the breaking occurs in the world of Nekudim when Lights enter Kelim that can't maintain the intention to bestow over the newly revealed depth of desire. The screen fails and the Kelim fall into reception for themselves.

This process is included in the Thought of Creation. It becomes the root of all later states and mixes qualities of Kedusha and Klipot.

Adam HaRishon

After the worlds are prepared, the common soul called Adam HaRishon is created. It includes all future individual souls as organs of one spiritual body.

The sin of Adam means an additional breaking of the bond in the common Kli. Each part then feels separate and can gradually come to the need for unity.

Egoism as a broken bond

In the spiritual sense, egoism isn't the desire to enjoy itself. It is the intention to use connection, Light, and others for oneself. Ordinary bodily needs aren't the object of spiritual correction by themselves.

The place of correction

The relation between parts of the common soul is corrected. Rejection and hatred are revealed precisely in connection, and above them the possibility of love is built.

Sparks of Light

After the breaking, sparks of bestowal remain inside the desires. Because of them a person can value connection, search for the meaning of life, and long for the Creator.

Klipot use these sparks for egoistic existence. For this reason outwardly good actions may be performed for honor, control, self comfort, or future reward.

Freedom of choice and prayer

The breaking creates room for choosing an environment. A person doesn't choose the initial desire, but can choose the influence of a society that raises the importance of bestowal.

Through inclusion in a ten, one gathers broken parts in a model of the common soul and awakens the reforming Light.

Formula of the work

Revelation of separation. Effort toward unity. Discovery of inability. A common request. The action of the Light. A new measure of connection.

Practical direction

The main error is trying to correct the friend. The breaking is revealed in me as my attitude toward the common bond. The work is to raise the friends, participate in the common effort, and ask the Creator to connect what we can't connect.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

The breaking was a mistake in the program.

Correct direction

It was included in the Thought of Creation as a condition for independent correction.

Don't understand it this way

Differences between people must be erased.

Correct direction

The bond is corrected above differences. Diversity increases the richness of the common Kli.

Don't understand it this way

Outwardly kind actions are automatically bestowal.

Correct direction

Spiritual quality is determined by intention and similarity with the Creator.

Lesson summary

  1. Intention and connection broke, not the substance of desire.
  2. The breaking mixed qualities of reception and bestowal.
  3. Adam HaRishon is the common system of souls.
  4. Egoism is revealed in the relation between parts of the common soul.
  5. Correction takes place through the environment, a request, and the action of the Light.
08The worlds of correction and the ascent of soulsOpen

Source reference: Items 112, 144, and 161

The world of Atzilut

After the breaking, the world of Atzilut is built as the system of correction and governance. It organizes Lights, screens, and Partzufim so broken souls can receive correction gradually.

The name Atzilut points to closeness to divinity. The intention to bestow governs there.

The worlds of BYA

Beria, Yetzira, and Assiya are degrees of distance from the revelation of the Creator. They describe states of souls before their full inclusion in the correction of Atzilut.

BYA aren't storage places for souls. They are degrees of desire's relation to bestowal.

How correction takes place

Souls raise a lack to Atzilut. In response, the system transmits correcting Light. After acquiring an intention to bestow, desires can ascend and be included in Kedusha.

Two movements

The ascent of MAN is the lower one's request for correction. The descent of MAD is the response of the upper system as Light and the force of correction.

Revelation of the Creator in connection

The Creator is revealed not inside an isolated person but in the corrected bond between parts of the common soul. The place of revelation is called Shechina, the gathering of corrected desires directed toward unity.

The more people build bestowal above rejection, the larger the Kli for revelation of the Creator's quality.

There is no disappearance in spirituality

A new state doesn't destroy the previous one. Every attained form remains as the foundation of the next. It is like one candle lighting another without losing its own flame.

Ascent means adding a new attainment, not erasing the past.

Six thousand degrees

The expression six thousand years points primarily to the complete order of correction of desire. In spiritual language, time means a change of states. It describes the complete ascent of Malchut toward similarity with the Creator.

Spiritual time

Spiritual time is measured by renewals of form. A change of intention is movement. Lack of inner change is a stop in spiritual time.

Theory and practice

Studying the structure of the worlds draws Surrounding Light and gives a correct map. Knowledge of diagrams by itself isn't spiritual attainment.

Practice means building similarity to the system through connection, mutual responsibility, importance of the goal, and turning to the Creator. Theory without connection remains information. Practice without sources easily turns into psychology or morality.

Final correction

The final goal is for every desire, including the deepest, to receive the correct intention and be able to receive all the Light in order to bestow. Then the Thought of Creation is fulfilled without shame and separation.

The created being doesn't disappear in the Creator. It preserves independent desire and reaches similarity of form.

Practical direction

Study should end not with satisfaction from understanding but with an appeal to the goal. Ask what connection this law describes, what prevents it from being revealed between us, and what common request can invite the force of correction.

Common misunderstandings

Don't understand it this way

Ascent to Atzilut means leaving ordinary life.

Correct direction

Ascent is a change of inner quality while ordinary life continues.

Don't understand it this way

Knowing the names of the worlds is attainment.

Correct direction

Attainment means actually acquiring the quality of bestowal.

Don't understand it this way

Personal perfection is the final goal.

Correct direction

Correction is completed through restoration of the common Kli and revelation of the Creator in the bond of all its parts.

Lesson summary

  1. Atzilut is the governing system of correction.
  2. The worlds of BYA express degrees of concealment and states of souls.
  3. The lower ones raise a request and the upper system transmits correcting Light.
  4. The Creator is revealed in corrected connection.
  5. Theory must be joined with the practice of unity.
  6. Final correction fulfills the Thought of Creation in the entire desire.

One formula for the whole wisdom

Light and desire

Light creates desire. Desire senses the Giver. It discovers opposition. It restricts egoistic reception. It acquires a screen. It receives in order to bestow. It passes through breaking. It corrects the bond. It reaches complete similarity with the Creator.

Connection with the work in the ten

All the technical details of Pticha describe the inner mechanics of correcting the common soul. The ten is a practical model of this Kli. In it a person discovers resistance to connection, the need to restrict the private calculation, the lack of a screen, and dependence on the correcting Light.

  1. The environment gives importance to the goal.
  2. The attempt to unite reveals the breaking.
  3. Self annulment creates room for the common Kli.
  4. Shared study draws Surrounding Light.
  5. Prayer expresses a lack for the force of bestowal.
  6. Corrected connection becomes the place of revelation of the Creator.

Main caution

Don't directly attribute spiritual degrees, screens, and Lights to yourself. Before real attainment, the terms are studied as the structure of the upper system. The practical conclusion is to move toward connection and ask for correction.

Conclusion

Pticha leads from the question of the substance of creation to the complete system of correction of the common soul. Its central idea remains constant. The will to receive stays, but it acquires an intention to bestow. The created being isn't erased. It becomes an independent partner of the Creator and receives all the prepared delight in similarity of form.
